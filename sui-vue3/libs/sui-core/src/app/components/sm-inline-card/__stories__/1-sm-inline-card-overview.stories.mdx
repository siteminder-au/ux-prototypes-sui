## Usage
Inline cards should be used in scenarios where users need to be made aware of some information, but the information does not impact the workflow, or, the information is relevant; but inserting it inline with other content would break the visual flow of the page.

For example:

- When inside a booking information drawer, a message is needed to notify the information failed to sync to other platforms.
- Long user agreement forms, that users have to click a checkbox to agree to, but will break visual flow if the whole agreement is inserted into the page


## Formatting

### Anatomy
**1: Container -** Container size is defined by the amount of space those elements take up. The container expresses card elevation.

**2: Icon (for info, alert, warning and success types) -** Supplementary visual to aid content

**3: Title -** The subject of the card, frequently communicated through the headline text.

**4: Subtext -** Body material, such as an article summary or a description, is an example of supporting text.

**5: Body -** Content area used to display long form text or other custom data

**6: Footer (optional) -** Section used for inputs/actions

**7: Close (optional) -** Action to close the card

**8: Divider -** Used to separate heading, body and footer.

### Placement
Inline cards can be placed in a wizard, or the drawer, or anywhere on the page where users need to be made aware of some information, but the information does not impact the workflow on the page.

#### Inline cards in a wizard or anywhere in a page
Inline cards can be place in a wizard or on a page. Make sure to follow the breakpoints guidelines and the specs in the wizard. Recommended maximum width in a wizard is **600px**.

#### Inline cards in a drawer
When used in drawers, inline card will fill the minimum **container width of 464px**, observing the drawer padding rules.
