## Accessibility

Please keep accessibility in mind when choosing colours, as it is our responsibility to deliver a product that's inclusive to everyone.

The Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) specify specific ratios that achieve the minimum required contrast for legibility.

- Small text is any size **below 24px** and requires a **4.5:1 contrast ratio**.
- Large text is anything **above 24px** and requires a **3:1 contrast ratio**.
- Graphical elements, such as data visualizations, also require a **3:1 contrast ratio**.

### Protanopia (Rare)

People with protanopia are unable to perceive any 'red' light. Affects about 2.5% of all males. Protanopes are more likely to confuse:
- Black with many shades of red
- Dark brown with dark green, dark orange, dark red, dark blue/purple and black
- Some blues with some reds, purples and dark pinks
- Mid-greens with some oranges

<img src={colorBlind2} alt="Protanopia" width="846" />

### Deuteranopia (Common)

Deuteranomaly is the most common type of red-green colour blindness. It makes green look more red. Affects about 5% of all males.

Deuteranopes are more likely to confuse:
- Mid-reds with mid-greens
- Blue-greens with grey and mid-pinks; Bright greens with yellows
- Pale pinks with light grey/white ; Mid-reds with mid-brown
- Light blues with lilac

<img src={colorBlind3} alt="Deuteranomaly" width="846" />


### Tritanopia (Very Rare)

People with tritanopia make you unable to tell the difference between blue and green, purple and red, and yellow and pink. It also makes colours look less bright.
Affect about 0.5% of all males.The most common colour confusions for tritanopes are light blues with greys, dark purples with black,
mid-greens with blues and oranges with reds.

<img src={colorBlind4} alt="Tritanopia" width="846" />

### Monochromacy / Achromatopsia (Extremely rare)

People with monochromatic vision can see no colour at all and their world consists of different shades of grey ranging from black to white,
rather like seeing the world on an old black and white television set. Achromatopsia is a specific eye condition in which people see in greyscale.
